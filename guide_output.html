ALEX's Audition Playbook

Project Overview

This is a single-camera Disney sitcom, likely aimed at a teen/tween audience. The tone will be grounded and emotionally honest, with relatable family dynamics and humor that comes from the characters rather than gags. Think "The Middle" meets "Speechless" - genuine coming-of-age stories with a touch of quirk. Comparable Projects: - "The Middle" (realistic family struggles with heart) - "Speechless" (chaotic household navigating challenges) - "Fresh Off the Boat" (cultural identity through teen's eyes) - "Atypical" (navigating adolescence with unique POV) - "Malcolm in the Middle" (clever teen protagonist with complicated home life) The Scene Context: This is a pivotal moment between Alex and her cop mom, Jen. Alex has found herself in legal trouble again, and Jen is trying to understand what's really going on. The casting team wants to see an actor who can balance the rebellious, jaded side of Alex with the vulnerable, hurt teenager underneath. They need to believe this is a real, complicated mother-daughter relationship, not just a one-note conflict.

Character Breakdown

Alex is a 15-year-old rebel with a lot of walls. Underneath the tough, sarcastic exterior is a kid who just wants her mom to see her - the real her, not just the "problem child" persona. She's been through a lot, from her dad's death to her own brushes with the law, and she's built up a thick shell to protect herself. But there's a passionate, feeling person in there waiting to be understood. How Alex Sees Herself: A screw-up who can't do anything right. Someone who's always disappointing her mom and letting people down. A lost cause who might as well just embrace being a juvenile delinquent because no one believes she can be anything else. How Others See Her: Jen sees a troubled kid who's acting out, but she's so focused on protecting her own reputation that she keeps missing the real issues. The cops and probation officers see a repeat offender. Her teachers probably see a smart but disruptive student. No one sees the wounded, sensitive soul underneath all the bravado. Your Bridge to Alex: - Have you ever felt misunderstood by the adults in your life? - Do you use humor and attitude as a defense mechanism? - Have you struggled to communicate your true feelings to someone close to you? - Can you relate to the experience of losing a parent at a young age? - Do you know what it's like to try your best but still get labeled as a "problem child"? The Empathy Stretch: Alex has been through a lot more trauma than the average teenager. She's lost her father, been in trouble with the law, and feels constantly at odds with her mom - the one person who's supposed to have her back. Tap into your own experiences with grief, guilt, and feeling like an outsider in your own family. Character Archetype Comparables: - The Wounded Teen with Walls ("Kayla" from Euphoria) - The Young Rebel with a Golden Heart ("Rue" from Euphoria) - The Grieving Teenager ("Tess" from This Is Us) - The Misunderstood Child of a Cop ("Dex" from The Middle) - The Smart-Mouthed Survivor ("Frankie" from The Middle)

UTA Hagen's 9 Questions (Alex Edition)

  1. Who am I? I'm Alex, a 15-year-old girl who's been through way too much for her age. I'm a smart-mouthed rebel who uses sarcasm as armor, but deep down I just want my mom to see me for who I really am instead of always expecting the worst.
  2. Where am I? In the front seat of my mom's cop car, somewhere in our neighborhood. This is familiar territory, but I still feel trapped and out of place - like I can never escape the same old cycle.
  3. What time is it? Late at night, after some kind of incident that's landed me in trouble again. The adrenaline is still pumping, but the exhaustion of constantly disappointing everyone is starting to set in.
  4. What surrounds me? The interior of the cop car, which feels cold and impersonal. The streetlights and houses passing by are a reminder of the "normal" life I can never seem to achieve. I'm hyper-aware of the police radio squawking in the background.
  5. What are the relationships? This is a fraught, complicated mother-daughter relationship. Jen is the cop who's supposed to protect me, but she's also the parent who's let me down the most. We're at odds, but there's an undercurrent of love and worry that makes this interaction so much more charged.
  6. What are the circumstances? I'm on probation, which means I'm one screw-up away from juvie. Jen caught me at the scene of some kind of incident, and now she's trying to figure out what really happened - even though she's usually only interested in protecting her own reputation as an officer.
  7. What do I want? To be understood. To have Jen see me as a whole person, not just a problem she has to fix. I want her to listen and really hear me for once, instead of just shutting me down.
  8. What's in my way? My own walls and defense mechanisms. The fact that I've lied to Jen so many times before, so she has a hard time believing me now. The burden of always having to prove myself and the exhaustion of constantly fighting an uphill battle.
  9. How do I get what I want? I have to find a way to break through Jen's cop-mode defenses and make her see the real me. Maybe I start out sarcastic and deflective, then let my guard down in a moment of genuine vulnerability. Or I try to appeal to her as a parent, reminding her that I'm still just a kid who needs her mom.

Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

Emotional Arc: Alex starts out defiant and guarded, then gradually drops her tough-girl act as she finally opens up about the hurt and frustration she's been carrying.

Scene 1:

- **Action**: Alex reluctantly gets into Jen's car, making it clear she'd rather be anywhere else. - **Subtext**: "I'm sick of having to explain myself to you. Why even bother?" - **Physicality**: Tense, defensive body language; arms crossed, eyes averted. - **Action**: Jen tries to engage Alex, but Alex responds with sarcasm and attitude. - **Subtext**: "I'm not going to make this easy for you. You've never been there for me before." - **Physicality**: Animated, almost performative; Alex is putting on a "tough girl" act. - **Action**: Alex finally opens up about how she feels Jen has let her down. - **Subtext**: "You were my mom first, but you've always chosen your job over me. I needed you and you weren't there." - **Physicality**: The sarcasm and bravado start to crack; Alex is more vulnerable and emotional.

Scene 2:

- **Action**: Jen tries to get Alex to explain what really happened, but Alex deflects and lashes out. - **Subtext**: "I'm so tired of having to prove myself to you. You never believe me anyway." - **Physicality**: Agitated, almost pacing at times; Alex is trying to keep up her defenses. - **Action**: Alex finally breaks down and admits the truth about what happened, letting her guard down. - **Subtext**: "I'm so scared of disappointing you again, but I can't keep living with this guilt. Please just listen to me for once." - **Physicality**: Shoulders slumping, voice cracking with emotion; Alex is emotionally drained.

Physicality & Movement

Alex carries herself with a swagger and defensiveness that belies her underlying vulnerability. Her body language is tense and guarded - arms crossed, chin up, eyes narrowed. But when her walls start to come down, she becomes more open and expressive, with greater use of her hands and a softening of her features. Vocally, Alex has a quick, biting delivery laced with sarcasm. Her accent and speech patterns should feel authentic to a working-class teen from this setting. When she's being more vulnerable, her volume might drop and her voice could tremble slightly. In terms of framing, keep the camera relatively tight on Alex, capturing her face and upper body. This will allow you to really hone in on her emotional shifts and reactions. Avoid wide shots that would diminish the intensity of the scene.

Subtext & Emotional Layers

Key Line: "Your 'Officer Mom' routine might play at the station but it doesn't play here." Subtext: "I'm sick of you only seeing me as a problem you have to fix, instead of a human being who's hurting. This isn't about being the good cop; it's about being my mom." Key Line: "I told you I didn't do it." Subtext: "Why don't you ever believe me? Haven't I proven myself enough times? I'm so tired of constantly having to defend myself to you." Key Line: "Mother's intuition?" Subtext: (Means to sting) "If you really knew me, you'd know I'm telling the truth. But you've stopped trying to understand me a long time ago." The Trap to Avoid: Playing Alex as a one-note angry rebel. She's complex - the sarcasm and attitude are a defense mechanism, not her true self. Your Secret Weapon: Lean into Alex's vulnerability and hurt. The audience will connect with her if they see the genuine pain and longing for her mom's understanding.

"I just wanted you to be there for me, Mom." 2. **The Sarcastic Softening**: After a particularly biting remark, Alex pauses and lets a hint of a smile slip through, almost surprised by her own ability to be playful. 3. **The Quiet Desperation**: Rather than explosive anger, what if Alex's reactions are more subdued and despairing? "I'm so tired of trying..." Genre-Specific Strategy: This is a character-driven, grounded comedy. Avoid broad, over-the-top choices. Focus on authenticity and emotional truth - the humor will come from Alex's relatable struggles and the unique way she processes them. The Audition Trap: Trying too hard to "act tough." Remember, the toughness is a defense mechanism. Peel back the layers and let the vulnerability shine through.
