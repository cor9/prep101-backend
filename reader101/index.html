WHAT YOU GET

Not reader tips. Reader coaching.

01

Performance Engine

We tell you whether this scene runs on push or pull — and exactly when to shift. Get the energy type wrong and your actor plays the wrong scene.

02

Key Beats

Most readers blow past the silence your kid needs. We tell you exactly where to shut up, and what collapses if you don't.

03

Reader Fundamentals

The 10 rules every reader breaks without noticing. The 50% rule, the 3-foot rule, how to listen on camera, when to step away.

04

Do This / Avoid This

Scene-specific, not generic. "If you rush this line, the actor loses the emotional turn." This scene only. No filler.

05

Connection Coaching

Dead eyes are visible on camera. We tell you exactly when connection is non-negotiable, and how to stay in it without performing.

06

Quick Reset

Four to five punchy reminders to read right before you press record. So you walk in focused, not guessing.

07

Playing Multiple Characters

Most scenes have 2–4 voices one reader has to cover. We tell you how to make each one distinct — because if two characters sound the same, the audition dies instantly.

08

What Will Go Wrong

Every guide opens with the three ways readers kill this specific audition. You see the landmines before you walk through them.

WHY IT WORKS

Every other reader tip looks like this.
Reader101 looks like this.

"
Generic advice
  • "Don't rush the lines."
  • "Be supportive."
  • "Match the actor's energy."
  • "Don't read ahead."
"
Reader101
  • "At the honeymoon spiral, freeze. Don't laugh. Your stillness is the scene."
  • "When Ben rewrites your nickname to 'Gare With No Hair' — take the hit. Don't laugh it off."
  • "This is a pull scene until line 14, then it flips. If you stay soft, the turn never lands."
  • "Hold brief eye contact on 'Do not poo-poo the poof.' This is real wisdom being dispensed."
