# CHARACTER ARCHETYPE COMPARABLES

## The Accidentally Brutal Honest Kid
- Leslie Knope (Parks & Rec) — aggressive positivity that bulldozes social cues
- Kate McKinnon's SNL recurring characters — specific, committed, slightly unhinged but sincere
- Buddy the Elf — no filter, pure sincerity, doesn't know he's funny
- Luna Lovegood (extroverted version) — speaks truth others won't

## The Dreamy Quirky Outsider
- Phoebe Buffay (Friends) — spacey wisdom, internal logic
- Kenneth Parcell (30 Rock) — earnest oddball with complete conviction
- Luna Lovegood (Harry Potter) — alternative reality that makes sense to her
- Amélie — sees magic in the mundane

## The Wounded Teen with Walls
- Rue (Euphoria) — trauma masked by humor/drugs
- Steve Harrington (Stranger Things) — cool kid hiding depth
- Holden Caulfield — moral superiority masking pain
- Elio (Call Me By Your Name) — intelligence as defense mechanism

## The Fish Out of Water
- Ted Lasso — competent in the wrong environment
- Alexis Rose (Schitt's Creek) — sheltered, learning to adapt
- Elle Woods (Legally Blonde) — underestimated but capable
- David Rose (Schitt's Creek) — sophistication in an unsophisticated setting

## The Tiny CEO / Confident Kid
- Eloise — rules her domain, expects to be taken seriously
- Matilda — intelligence beyond years
- Wednesday Addams — dry confidence, unbothered
- Kevin McCallister (Home Alone) — takes charge of the situation

## The Narrator / Chronicler
- Nick Carraway (The Great Gatsby) — observer who becomes participant
- Kvothe (Name of the Wind) — telling his own mythologized story
- JD (Scrubs) — internal monologue reveals truth
- Ferris Bueller — fourth-wall breaking confidence

## The Survivor Who Chooses Kindness
- Beth (Little Women) — soft strength
- Fern (Nomadland) — grief channeled into presence
- Luna Lovegood — bullied but unbowed
- Amélie — trauma transformed into magic-making
